ABOUT

Chinese traditional culture refers to the cultural traditions and customs formed during the thousands of years of historical development in China. It includes rich philosophical ideas, moral concepts, religious beliefs, art forms, etiquette systems, customs and habits, and other aspects. It is a manifestation of the Chinese national spirit and an important foundation for cultural confidence and identity of the Chinese nation.

The following are some of the main aspects of traditional Chinese culture: philosophical thinking, moral ethics, religious beliefs, literature and art, festival customs, etiquette systems, traditional medicine, architecture, clothing, food culture, etc. Chinese traditional culture not only has a profound impact on the values and lifestyle of Chinese people, but also has made important contributions to the diversity of world culture and the development of human civilization. With the development of the times, traditional Chinese culture is constantly integrating with modern culture, forming new forms of cultural expression.

Chinese philosophical thought: Represented by Confucianism, Taoism, Legalism, Mohism, and other ancient Chinese philosophical ideas, it has had a profound impact on Chinese culture.
Traditional Chinese clothing, such as Hanfu and Tang costumes, reflects the social, cultural, and aesthetic concepts of different historical periods.
Traditional Chinese architecture: Ancient Chinese buildings such as palaces, temples, gardens, etc. embody unique aesthetics and philosophical ideas.
Chinese medicine, such as traditional Chinese medicine, emphasizes the balance of yin and yang and the holistic concept.
Chinese traditional festivals: such as the Spring Festival, Mid-Autumn Festival, Loong Boat Festival, Tomb Sweeping Day, etc., each festival has its own specific way of celebration and cultural significance.
Chinese traditional art: Chinese literature and art have a long history, including various forms such as poetry, prose, novels, drama, painting, calligraphy, music, dance, etc.
Religious belief: including Buddhism, Taoism, Confucianism, etc. These religions and philosophical thoughts influence each other and together form the Chinese world outlook and outlook on life.
Etiquette: The ancient etiquette system strictly regulated people's behavior, such as weddings, funerals, sacrifices, etc.
Food culture: Chinese food culture is rich and diverse, with distinctive dishes and dietary habits in different regions.
